Conside the PDE of the function \(f=f(x,t)\):

\[ \dot{f}=\nabla\left(D\nabla f\right)+p \]

Using the general- \(\theta\) approximation the time discretization:

\[ \frac{f_{n+1}-f_n}{dt}=\theta\left[\nabla(D\nabla f_{n+1})+p_{n+1}\right]+(1-\theta) \left[\nabla(D\nabla f_{n})+p_{n}\right] \]

Let \(\delta\) be the test operator, and \((X,Y)=\int_\Omega XY\, d\Omega\) indicate integration over the domain \(\Omega\). The Galerkin form of the equation above is obtained by multiplying it by \(\delta f\), and integration over the domain:

\[ (\delta f,f_{n+1})=(\delta f,f_{n}) +(\delta f,dt\,\theta\,\nabla(D\nabla f_{n+1}))+(\delta f,dt\,\theta\,p_{n+1}) +(\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,\nabla(D\nabla f_n))+(\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,p_n) \]

We can reduce the order of differentiation in the second and ourth terms on the rhs using integration by parts. This leads to:

\[ (\delta f,f_{n+1})=(\delta f,f_{n}) +\langle\delta f,dt\,\theta\,D\nabla f_{n+1}\cdot N\rangle-(\nabla\delta f,dt\,\theta\,D\nabla f_{n+1})+(\delta f,dt\,\theta\,p_{n+1}) +\langle\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,D\nabla f_n\cdot N\rangle-(\nabla\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,D\nabla f_n)+(\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,p_n) \]

Rearranging:

\[ \underbrace{ (\delta f,f_{n+1}) -\langle\delta f,dt\,\theta\,D\nabla f_{n+1}\cdot N\rangle +(\nabla\delta f,dt\,\theta\,D\nabla f_{n+1}) }_{\mathbf{A}\mathbf{f}_{n+1}} =\underbrace{ (\delta f,f_{n}) +\langle\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,D\nabla f_n\cdot N\rangle-(\nabla\delta f,dt\,(1-\theta)\,D\nabla f_n)+(\delta f,dt\,\theta\,p_{n+1}+dt\,(1-\theta)\,p_n) }_{\mathbf{b}_n} \]

Here we used th divergence theorem and introduced the boundary integral \(<X,Y>=\int_{\partial\Omega} XY\, d\partial\Omega\). Here the trial function is \(f_{n+1}\), while \(f_n\) is known. Therefore

  • the lhs is a weak form bilinear in the test funtion and trial function.
  • the rhs is a weak forms linear in the test function.

Minimal code:

Upon FEM discretization, the bilinear weak forms gives rise to the "stiffness" matrix \(\mathbf{A}\), while the linear weak forms contribute to the "nodal force vector" \(\mathbf{b}\). The solution is found solving the system of equations

\[ \mathbf{A}\mathbf{f}_{n+1}=\mathbf{b}_n \]
